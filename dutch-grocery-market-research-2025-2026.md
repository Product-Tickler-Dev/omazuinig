## 2. CONSUMER BEHAVIOR

### Store Visits
- **Average supermarket visits: ~3 times per week** per Dutch consumer
- Average time per visit: ~30 minutes
- **33% of consumers changed their main supermarket** in the past year, with half citing inflation/cost-of-living as the reason
- **58% of consumers** say their grocery shopping behavior has changed due to price increases
- Cross-border shopping to Germany/Belgium/Luxembourg has **increased 5x in one year**

### Price Sensitivity
- **87% of consumers are concerned about rising grocery prices** (Deloitte 2025 survey, n=2,261)
- **74% have adjusted purchasing behavior** - opting for cheaper products or those on special offer
- Price, convenience, and sustainability are the top three factors influencing supermarket choice
- Budget-friendliness and discounts are the **most decisive drivers across ALL generations**
- Shoppers invest more time in studying weekly folders/flyers to plan meals
- Discounters (Aldi, Lidl) continue gaining share as shoppers seek lower prices
- More than **50% of Dutch consumers use supermarket apps** multiple times per week
- **73% of Dutch consumers use the Albert Heijn app** (most popular); Jumbo second at 34%

### Private Label vs. A-Brand Split
- **Private label accounts for 42-47% of grocery retail sales** in the Netherlands (one of the highest in Europe)
- Private label is **steadily gaining** market share; A-brands are losing ground
- Price gap between A-brands and private labels has expanded to **nearly 40%**
- Dutch supermarkets are introducing **two-tier private label strategies**: one for price, one for quality
- Hard discount formats (Aldi, Lidl) are driving much of this shift
- Netherlands is among Europe's top 3 markets for private label penetration (alongside Spain and Germany)

### Loyalty Programs
- Netherlands loyalty market valued at **$1 billion in 2025**, projected to reach $1.28B by 2029
- Dutch loyalty programs are shifting from long-term points to **instant discounts and digital vouchers**
- AH Bonuskaart and Jumbo Extra's are the dominant loyalty ecosystems
- Loyalty is being used as a **cost-control and retention tool**, not just branding

## 3. EXISTING PRICE COMPARISON APPS & TOOLS

### 3.1 Supermarktaanbiedingen.com / Supermarkt+ App

| Attribute | Detail |
|---|---|
| **What it does** | Aggregates weekly offers/deals from ALL Dutch supermarkets. Users can search by product, brand, or store. Includes shopping list functionality, store locator, and opening times. |
| **Users** | Market leader in displaying supermarket offers for several years. Exact user count not publicly disclosed. |
| **Business model** | Ad-supported; affiliate links to supermarket websites. |
| **Platforms** | Website + iOS/Android app ("Supermarkt+") |
| **User complaints** | Mixed reviews; lack of local search functionality by location; some users uninstall due to this. App is comprehensive but perceived as basic/utilitarian. |
| **Competitive position** | Strong for browsing weekly deals/folders, but NOT a true price comparison tool (shows promotions, not full price databases). |

### 3.2 SmartKoop

| Attribute | Detail |
|---|---|
| **What it does** | True price comparison across AH, Jumbo, Lidl, Aldi, Plus, and others. Smart Shopping List shows which store offers the best value for your full basket. Also includes AI Nutrition Coach (Nutri-Score analysis). |
| **Users** | Relatively new entrant; growing. Exact numbers not publicly disclosed. |
| **Business model** | Free app; likely ad-supported + potential premium features. |
| **Platforms** | iOS + Android + web (smart-koop.nl) |
| **Key features** | Real-time price comparison, flyer aggregation, AI health coach, cheaper alternative suggestions |
| **User complaints** | Limited public data on complaints. As a newer app, likely smaller product database than established players. |
| **Competitive position** | Closest direct competitor to the Oma Zuinig concept. Functional but "no soul" - lacks emotional branding or personality. |

### 3.3 Checkjebon

| Attribute | Detail |
|---|---|
| **What it does** | Open-source, browser-based tool that calculates total shopping list cost per supermarket. Strong privacy focus - ALL data processing happens locally in the browser. |
| **Users** | Niche/tech-savvy audience. Open-source community project, not a commercial product. |
| **Business model** | Non-commercial; open-source (GitHub: supermarkt/checkjebon). No ads, no tracking, no revenue model. |
| **Platforms** | Web only (checkjebon.nl) |
| **Key features** | Total basket cost comparison, sharable shopping lists, zero data collection (localStorage only), open data reusable by other projects |
| **User complaints** | Limited UI/UX; web-only (no native app); likely incomplete price database; no barcode scanning. |
| **Competitive position** | Privacy-first tool beloved by tech community. Data may serve as a reusable resource for other projects. Not a mainstream consumer app. |

### 3.4 Btrkoop

| Attribute | Detail |
|---|---|
| **What it does** | Combines price comparison with receipt scanning and a rewards/points system. Scans receipts to give smart suggestions for cheaper alternatives. Price alerts for favorited products. |

### 3.5 Scoupy

### 3.7 Other Relevant Tools

| Tool | Description |
|---|---|
| **Dyme** | Budgeting app (not grocery comparison). Auto-categorizes bank transactions, finds better deals on fixed contracts. Average user saves EUR 800/year. Free + premium tiers. Very popular in NL. |
| **Alle Folders / Folders Online** | Digital supermarket flyer aggregators. Show weekly deals but don't compare prices product-by-product. |

### Competitive Landscape Summary

**Critical gap in the market:**
- Supermarktaanbiedingen = deals aggregation (not true comparison)
- Scoupy/CashbackXL = cashback (not comparison)
- SmartKoop = closest to true comparison, but no personality/brand
- Checkjebon = open-source/privacy-first, but niche
- Btrkoop = hybrid comparison + rewards, small

**No app combines: true cross-store price comparison + emotional branding + gamification + barcode scanning in a single, beloved consumer product.** This is the Oma Zuinig opportunity.

## 4. DUTCH GROCERY DELIVERY LANDSCAPE

### Online Grocery Market Overview
- Online grocery sales in the Netherlands: **~EUR 1.7 billion** (2024), representing **3-4% of total supermarket turnover**
- AH registers ~10% of its sales online
- Online grocery shoppers increased by 10% to ~22% of consumers
- Consumers who shop both in-store and online buy **47% of groceries online**

### Major Players

#### Picnic (Online-Only)
- **Online market share: ~39%** (2023, surpassed AH as #1 online grocer)
- Only Dutch supermarket that exclusively operates online (founded 2015)
- Raised **EUR 430M Series E** in November 2025
- 5,415 employees as of early 2026
- Expanding aggressively into Germany
- Competitive pricing model with regular delivery routes (no delivery fee for minimum orders)

#### Albert Heijn Online (ah.nl)
- **Online market share: ~37%** (2023)
- Multiple delivery options: home delivery, pickup points
- Integrated with physical stores and AH app ecosystem
- Leveraging Bonuskaart loyalty for online retention

#### Jumbo Online (jumbo.com)
- **Online market share: ~22%** (2023)
- Home delivery + pickup at stores
- Less aggressive online than AH/Picnic

#### Flink (Rapid/Q-Commerce)
- **Sole remaining rapid delivery operator in NL** (Getir/Gorillas exited)
- Present in **40+ municipalities, ~60 locations** in Netherlands
- 10% of Rotterdam households are regular users
- Raised **$100M Series C in March 2026** at higher valuation
- Planning expansion from 80 to 110 cities (DE + NL) by 2027
- Achieved country-by-country profitability
- Dark store regulations remain a challenge in some Dutch cities

#### Getir / Gorillas - EXITED
- Getir confirmed exit from Netherlands in April 2024
- Gorillas (acquired by Getir) also shut down
- Left Flink as sole rapid delivery player

### Impact on Price Comparison
- Online-only players like Picnic have **transparent, fixed pricing** (no in-store-only deals) - easier to scrape/compare
- Delivery fees and minimum order thresholds complicate true price comparison
- AH online prices generally match in-store; Picnic aims for Lidl-level pricing
- Flink typically charges **premium prices** (convenience markup)
- A comprehensive price comparison app MUST include online delivery options to be complete
- Different pricing between online/offline for the same chain creates confusion that a comparison tool can solve

## 5. RECENT TRENDS

### 5.1 Inflation Impact on Shopping Behavior

**Key data points (2025):**
- Dutch grocery prices **7% higher YoY** as of January 2025
- By November 2025, food inflation moderated to **3.1%** but still above EU average
- Dutch grocery prices rose **3.7% YoY in September 2025** vs. EU average of 3%
- Netherlands grocery inflation **consistently higher than neighboring countries**

**Behavioral changes:**
- 58% of consumers changed shopping behavior due to price rises
- 74% opt for cheaper products or specials
- 33% switched their primary supermarket
- Cross-border shopping to Germany/Belgium increased **5x in one year**
- Weekly folders/flyers studied more carefully for meal planning
- Discounters (Aldi, Lidl) are the biggest market share winners
- Private label adoption accelerating

### 5.2 Shrinkflation Awareness

- **74% of European consumers** have noticed products getting smaller without price decreases (EU Consumer Conditions Scoreboard 2025)
- In the Netherlands, shrinkflation is making price increases "feel even worse"
- Dutch Consumentenbond demands brands announce packaging changes the same way they announce price increases
- Parliamentary debates underway in NL on how to address shrinkflation
- Netherlands currently has **no specific shrinkflation legislation** (unlike France, Italy, Hungary, Romania which do)
- Unit pricing exists but is considered insufficient since consumers rarely compare unit prices for regular purchases

### 5.3 Price Transparency & Regulation

**ACM (Authority for Consumers & Markets) Investigation:**
- ACM launched a **formal market investigation into supermarket pricing** in late 2025
- Focus: price breakdowns and price formations for average grocery basket
- Results expected by **end of 2026**
- This is a direct response to consumer complaints about opaque pricing

**Omnibus Directive / Price Indication Rules:**
- Crossed-out (discount) prices must show the **lowest price from the prior 30 days**
- Violations can result in fines up to **EUR 900,000**
- ACM published new guidelines on price indications and comparisons

**Pricing Errors Discovery:**
- Consumentenbond investigation (Feb-Mar 2025): **13% of surveyed items had incorrect pricing**
- Most errors: prices higher at register than advertised
- Chains surveyed: DekaMarkt, Dirk, Hoogvliet, Nettorama, Poiesz, Vomar
- This creates a clear use case for receipt-verification features in apps

### 5.4 Other Notable Trends

**Health & Transparency:**
- Functional beverages, plant-based snacks, immunity supplements see double-digit growth
- Clean-label claims and sourcing transparency are key purchase drivers
- SmartKoop already added an AI Nutrition Coach feature

**Loyalty Wars:**
- Supermarkets shifting to instant digital discounts (away from stamp cards)
- Loyalty used as retention/cost-control mechanism
- Creates data lock-in that makes cross-store comparison harder (personalized pricing)

**Retailer Ratings (YouGov 2025):**
- Lidl rated #1 nationally: 7.50/10
- Dirk #2: 7.32/10
- Albert Heijn #3: 7.27/10
- Freshness is the strongest differentiator; sustainability has growth potential

## 6. STRATEGIC IMPLICATIONS FOR OMA ZUINIG

Based on this research, the following market conditions strongly favor the Oma Zuinig concept:

1. **Massive price sensitivity** - 87% concerned about prices, 74% actively changing behavior. The market WANTS a price comparison tool.

2. **No beloved brand exists** - SmartKoop is the closest functional competitor but has zero emotional branding. Scoupy has 1M+ users but is cashback-only, not comparison.

3. **13% pricing errors at register** - A receipt-checking feature ("check je bon met Oma!") has a proven use case backed by Consumentenbond data.

4. **Regulatory tailwind** - ACM investigating pricing transparency; potential new shrinkflation rules. Being the consumer's advocate aligns with where regulation is heading.

5. **Private label at 42-47%** - Huge opportunity to help consumers compare private label across chains (AH Huismerk vs. Jumbo Huismerk vs. Lidl equivalent).

6. **3 visits/week = 3 opportunities for engagement** - High visit frequency supports daily-use app behavior.

7. **Coop-Plus merger and market consolidation** - Market is still shifting; consumers are re-evaluating loyalties. Perfect time to capture switching shoppers.

8. **Cross-border shopping 5x increase** - Potential future feature: compare Dutch prices with German border supermarkets.

9. **Online delivery complicates comparison** - Picnic vs. AH online vs. Flink pricing differs. A tool that normalizes this (including delivery fees) would be genuinely novel.

10. **Existing spec accuracy check** - The grok.txt spec states "AH holds ~38% share, Jumbo ~20%, consumers visit 2+ stores per week." This research confirms and refines: AH at 38.2%, Jumbo at 19.9%, visits at ~3/week. The spec's market framing is directionally correct.
